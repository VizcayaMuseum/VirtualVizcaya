2017 Barge

The Barge, playfully designed to look like a boat, is a breakwater built to protect the house and terraces from waves. Deering commissioned all of the sculptures from American artist Alexander Stirling Calder in 1915. The local limestone that covers the Barge is soft and porous. The salt water of Biscayne Bay, along with storm waves, have caused significant erosion, which threatens the stability of the statuary and decorative reliefs. The lower landing steps are submerged due to rising waters.

3D documentation enables Vizcaya to monitor the condition of the Barge over time. Virtual models digitally present the structure and the sculptures as they look today, as they appeared 100 years ago and can even anticipate how they might look in the future.
